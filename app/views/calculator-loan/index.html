Tuition Fee Loan

You could get a £9,250 Tuition Fee Loan each year to pay for your course.

If you get a Tuition Fee Loan, the amount you borrow will have 'compound interest' added to it. This is not yet set for September 2024 but is currently 7.1%.

Using the current rates and payment dates, this means that if you borrow £9,250 in September 2024, study full-time and do not pay anything back, in September 2025 there will be an extra £510 interest on your loan.

This interest rate changes when you stop studying, and can increase depdending on your income.

Interest rates change every 3 months - find out more in the student loans terms and conditions.

You will need to start paying back your Tuition Fee Loan and any other student finance loans when you stop studying and earn more than a certain amount in one job. This is currently £25,000 but can change - find out more in repaying your student loans.

Maintenance Loan

You could get a £13,002 Maintenance Loan to contribute towards your living costs.

If you get a Maintenance Loan, the amount you borrow will have 'compound interest' added to it. This is not yet set for September 2024 but is currently 7.1%. Using the current rates and payment dates, this means that if you borrow £13,002 in September 2024, study full-time and do not pay anything back, in September 2025 there will be an extra £652 interest on your loan.

This interest rate changes when you stop studying, and can increase depdending on your income.

Interest rates change every 3 months - find out more in the student loans terms and conditions.

You will need to start paying back your Tuition Fee Loan and any other student finance loans when you stop studying and earn more than a certain amount in one job. this is currently £25,000 but can change - find out more in repaying your student loans.

How your Maintenance Loan is calculated:

£13,002 (the maximum Maintenance Loan available)
- £0 (the amount you might not be eligible for, based on your answers)
= £13,002 (the amount you could be eligible for, based on your answers)

You can find out more about how Maintenance Loans are calculated.

You may get less Maintenance Loan in your final year.
